**Why are there so few images of treehouses built by kids?**

*Stage: budding*
*Tags:*
	+ *enshitification*
	+ *design process*
	+ *algorithm*

![treehouse](treehouse.png)

What do these treehouses have in common?

None of them were built by kids.

But let's change the search to "treehouse made by kids".

![treehouse-1](treehouse-1.png)

Yeah right. Not even the third example from the left was built by an adult keeping their controlling fingers out of it. Too perfect and too neat.

But this isn't about adults not letting kids build treehouses (which might or might not be true). It's about why it's so damn hard to find images of them. I remember doing a search for this maybe ten years ago, and my memory was that there were more pictures back then.

Consider that I'm sitting in front of my computer, grumbling like many other +50 white men who work up some keyboard rage and blame nearby modern phenomena like phones, games, movies, or whatever crap. And sure, it's probably got something to do with kids spending less time outdoors these days. They're less likely to run around in containers just looking for planks to build dangerously rickety tree forts.

![Treehouse by Bethany Weeks](treehouse-bethany.jpg)

But I choose to think that it's part of enshitification - how image searches have become more streamlined. How algorithms push forward picture after picture of conforming snowmen. I choose to direct my grumpiness towards that hole because I can't live with the thought that kids no longer build dangerously rickety treehouses in trees.

*Note:*
I did find some pictures eventually. A Flickr group called [Kids huts expedition](https://www.flickr.com/groups/kidhuts/) and one called [forgotten treehouses](https://www.flickr.com/groups/forgottentreehouses/). So maybe this story ends on a high note after all.
