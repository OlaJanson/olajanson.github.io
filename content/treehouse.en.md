![[treehouse.png]]
What do these huts have in common?

None of them are built by children.

But let's change the search to “treehouse made by kids”.
![[treehouse-1.png]]
Yeah right. Not even the example third from the left has the adult managed to keep their controlling fingers in check. Far too straight and tidy.

But this isn't about adults not letting children build huts (which may or may not be true) but why it's so incredibly difficult to find pictures of it. I remember doing a search maybe ten years ago. My memory was then that there were more pictures of children's huts ten years ago.

Consider that I'm sitting in front of my computer speculating wildly. Like many other +50 white men, I would work up some keyboard rage and blame adjacent modern phenomena like phones, games, movies or other crap. And sure, it probably has to do with children moving less in forests and fields today. Increasingly rarely running into dumpsters just to find planks to build deadly wooden creations in treetops. You know those that are completely life-threatening but so charged with children's anticipation and creative joy.

![[treehouse by Bethany Weeks 1.jpg]]

But I choose to think that it's part of enshitification – how image searches have become more streamlined. How algorithms push forward picture after picture with conformist mush. I choose to direct my old man rage in that direction because I can't live with the thought that children no longer build deadly huts in trees.

Note:
I finally found pictures. A Flickr group called [[https://www.flickr.com/groups/kidhuts/|Kids huts expedition]] and one called [[https://www.flickr.com/groups/forgottentreehouses/|forgotten treehouses]]. So maybe this story ends on a high note.
