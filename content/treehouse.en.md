![[treehouse.png]]
What do these treehouses have in common?

None of them were built by children.

But let's change the search to “treehouse made by kids”.
![[treehouse-1.png]]
Yeah right. Not even the third one from the left has the adult managed to keep their controlling fingers in check. Far too straight and tidy.

But this isn't about adults not letting children build treehouses (which may or may not be true) but rather why it's so incredibly difficult to find pictures of them. I remember doing a search about ten years ago. My memory then was that there were more pictures of children's treehouses ten years ago.

Consider that I'm sitting in front of my computer speculating. Like many other white men over 50, I would work up some keyboard rage and blame adjacent modern phenomena like phones, games, movies, or other crap. And yes, it probably has to do with children today moving less often in forests and fields. Increasingly rarely running into containers just to find planks to build dangerous wooden creations in treetops. You know, those that are utterly life-threatening but so charged with children's anticipation and creative joy.

![[treehouse by Bethany Weeks 1.jpg]]

But I choose to think it's part of enshitification – how image searches have become more streamlined. How algorithms push forward image after image of conforming fluff. I choose to direct my old man rage in that direction because I cannot live with the thought that children no longer build dangerous treehouses.

Note:
I eventually found pictures. A Flickr group called [[https://www.flickr.com/groups/kidhuts/|Kids huts expidition]] and one called [[https://www.flickr.com/groups/forgottentreehouses/|forgotten treehouses]]. So perhaps this story ends on a positive note.
