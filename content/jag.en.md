I am a Swiss Army knife, I usually say.

Or maybe that's not a perfect description of what I am?

With a Swiss Army knife, its versatility is its primary unique feature. The individual tool is not that impressive, but fitting them all into one single red unit is unique. And that's probably where I feel the analogy falls short. I remember as a child how annoyed I was that this red multi-tool couldn't be used in my treehouses – how the small saw it included was useless for cutting branches. How virtually every tool was frustratingly poor compared to its single-purpose variant.

No, I rather think I should describe myself as a toolbox. But that figurative metaphor doesn't have the same impact. It's too general. But it'll have to do for now. But what does the toolbox contain?

Pedagogy, I would argue, is a well-used tool. I have worked on and off as a teacher, mainly in vocational colleges but also at universities and primary schools. I work to the best of my ability with [[formativ bedömning]]. Right now, exclusively in online courses.

[[Designprocess]] and creative processes are something I have also spent many professional years with. Most recently as creative director at SKF. But before that, as a business developer at HiQ.

[[Speldesign]]. I have designed games - digital, board games, and role-playing games. Right now, it's mostly role-playing games. I constantly have a game project going but rarely finish.
